# Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees

## Scholarship Overview

- **Website:** https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/opportunities/opportunities-for-individuals/students/erasmus-mundus-joint-masters
- **Coverage:** Full tuition waiver + monthly living allowance of ~€1,400/month (~€33,600 over 2 years) + health insurance + travel/installation costs. Exact amounts vary by programme.
- **Eligibility for Brazilians:** Yes — open to students worldwide. Brazilian applicants are classified as "Partner Country" (non-EU) students, which often has dedicated scholarship slots (typically ~10 per programme for non-EU). Some programmes limit 2 scholars per nationality.
- **Deadline:** Varies by programme — typically between November and March for programmes starting September of the following year. Each programme has its own deadline.
- **Duration:** 2 years (120 ECTS), studying at 2-4 universities across different European countries.
- **Documents Required:** Varies by programme, but typically: bachelor's degree + transcripts, English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL), motivation letter, CV, 2 recommendation letters, passport copy.
- **Selection Criteria:** Academic excellence, motivation/fit with programme, professional experience, geographic diversity. Fabio's 10+ years of professional experience and international robotics background are strong differentiators.
- **Notes:** You can apply to up to 3 different Erasmus Mundus programmes in the same year. Each programme manages its own selection independently. No age limit. Cannot receive the scholarship twice.

## Matching Programs

### 1. Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Societies (AISS)

- **Country:** Estonia → Portugal → Finland (mobility across 3 countries)
- **Universities:** Tallinn University, Lusófona University (Porto), Tampere University
- **University Ranking:** Tampere University ~300-400 QS; Tallinn and Lusófona are smaller specialized institutions
- **Duration:** 2 years (4 semesters, 120 ECTS)
- **Language:** English
- **Format:** In-person with mobility across 3 countries
- **Program URL:** https://aissprogram.eu/
- **Why it matches:** This is the single best match for Fabio's interests. It explicitly covers **AI ethics**, **sustainability**, **human-centered AI**, **responsible innovation**, **social impact**, and **digital rights/equality** — hitting 6+ topics from his list. The programme trains students to use participatory design to create AI and data-driven sustainable solutions for societies, framed around the UN Sustainable Development Goals. Graduates receive 3 degrees (MA in Social Sciences from Tallinn, MSc in Sustainable Information Technologies from Lusófona, MSc in Social Science from Tampere).
- **Key courses:**
  - Sustainable Digital Societies
  - Data and AI Science (machine learning, data management for sustainable information societies)
  - Active Citizenship Studies & Participatory Design
  - Interdisciplinary Project course with real clients (industry, NGOs, municipalities)
  - Professional Placement + Master's Thesis
- **Notable faculty/research groups:** Partners include Citizen OS (civic tech NGO), Estonian Anthropocene Center, and local municipalities. The programme bridges social sciences and technology.
- **Alumni/career outcomes:** Programme is relatively new (first cohort ~2024). Targets roles in AI-for-good innovation, civic tech, sustainability consulting, public sector digital transformation.
- **Scholarships:** ~10 Erasmus Mundus scholarships per cohort
- **Application deadline:** February 2, 2026 (for September 2026 start). Next cycle opens December 2, 2025.
- **Fit for Fabio:** Excellent. His engineering background + AI innovation experience + social impact motivation is exactly the profile this programme seeks. His experience leading AI innovation teams at Accenture and international robotics competitions make him a strong candidate.

### 2. European Master in Law, Data and Artificial Intelligence (EMILDAI)

- **Country:** Ireland → Netherlands or Brazil or France or Spain → Italy (varies by track)
- **Universities:** Dublin City University (Ireland), Erasmus University Rotterdam (Netherlands), Fundação Getulio Vargas Rio (Brazil), Avignon Université (France), Universidad de León (Spain), Università di Pisa (Italy)
- **University Ranking:** Erasmus University Rotterdam ~176 QS; DCU ~471 QS; Università di Pisa ~389 QS; FGV is top-3 in Latin America for social sciences
- **Duration:** 2 years (4 semesters, 120 ECTS)
- **Language:** English (Tracks C and D); French (Track A); Spanish (Track B)
- **Format:** In-person with mobility across 2-3 countries
- **Program URL:** https://emildai.eu/
- **Why it matches:** Directly addresses **AI ethics and governance**, **AI policy and regulation**, **digital rights and equality**, and **responsible innovation** — 4 core topics from Fabio's list. The programme tackles the legal, technical, and ethical challenges of digitisation. Two tracks are particularly relevant:
  - **Track C (Fundamental Rights & Sustainability)** at Erasmus University Rotterdam — combines AI with human rights and sustainability
  - **Track D (Global Digital Regulations)** at FGV Rio — focuses on digital regulation from a Global South perspective
- **Key courses:**
  - Introduction to Law, Data and AI (Year 1 at DCU)
  - Fundamental Rights and Sustainability (Track C, Rotterdam)
  - Global Digital Regulations (Track D, FGV Rio)
  - Human-Centric and Accountable AI (Pisa, semester 4)
  - AI Law and Ethics (Pisa, semester 4)
- **Notable faculty/research groups:** 70+ public and private partner organizations for placements, seminars, and summer schools. Erasmus University Rotterdam has a strong reputation in human rights and governance research.
- **Alumni/career outcomes:** Data protection officers, AI policy specialists, compliance professionals, legal technologists, roles in EU institutions, tech companies, government, think tanks, international NGOs focused on digital regulation.
- **Scholarships:** Erasmus Mundus full scholarships available. Automatic consideration for merit scholarships when applying in Round 1.
- **Application deadline:** Round 1: January 25, 2026; Round 2: March 22, 2026 (for September 2026 start)
- **Fees (without scholarship):** €20,919/year for non-EU students
- **Fit for Fabio:** Very strong, especially Tracks C and D. **Important caveat:** The programme requires a bachelor's in law (Law stream) or computing (Computing stream). Fabio's Control and Automation Engineering degree may qualify for the Computing stream, but this should be confirmed with the programme. Track D at FGV Rio is interesting but means spending a semester in Brazil rather than Europe. Track C (Rotterdam) is the recommended choice.
