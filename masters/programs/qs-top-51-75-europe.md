## Detailed Analysis by University

---

### 1. University of Bristol (UK) — QS #51

**Scholarships:**
- **Think Big Scholarships**: £6,500 / £13,000 / £26,000 toward tuition fees ONLY (first year). NOT a full-ride.
- **GREAT Scholarships** (British Council): Available but partial.
- Living cost bursary available for some Faculty of Arts, Law and Social Sciences programmes.
- **Deadline:** 10 April 2026 for September 2026 entry.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes.

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **MSc Artificial Intelligence for Business** — includes units on AI Societies, Ethics and Futures; prepares for roles in Responsible AI / AI Governance Lead. Strong ethics integration.
- **MSc Management (CSR and Sustainability)** — focuses on responsible business, ethical and sustainability issues.
- **MSc Artificial Intelligence** — technical but includes ethics and societal impact modules.

**Verdict:** Good programmes but NO true full-ride scholarship. Maximum £26k covers tuition only; living costs (£12-15k/year in Bristol) not covered.

---

### 2. University of Amsterdam (Netherlands) — QS #53

**Scholarships:**
- **Amsterdam Merit Scholarship (AMS)**: Covers full tuition + €5,000 additional allowance for 1 year. For non-EU/EEA students. Deadline: 15 January 2026 (LIKELY PASSED).
- **Amsterdam Economics and Business Talent Fund**: Can cover full tuition + study costs for 1 year.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes (non-EU).

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **Advanced Master's in Technology Governance (Advanced LLM)** — 1-year programme covering AI regulation, platform power, infrastructure regulation, digital rights, fundamental rights. Combines law, economics, and social sciences. HIGHLY RELEVANT. Early applicant discount available.
- UvA is ranked top 5 continental European law schools with globally recognized expertise in AI ethics and platform governance.
- Institute for Information Law — 30+ years of experience, top 3 globally.

**Verdict:** EXCELLENT programme fit (Technology Governance). AMS scholarship is strong but not a true full-ride (€5k stipend is modest for Amsterdam living costs of ~€1,000-1,200/month). Note: AMS deadline was 15 January 2026 — may have passed. Check for other funding rounds.

### 3. London School of Economics (UK) — QS #56

**Scholarships:**
- **AKO Masters Scholarship**: 2 FULL scholarships (tuition + stipend) and 8 tuition-only scholarships. Specifically for students from **South Asia, Africa, or Latin America**. BRAZILIANS ELIGIBLE. Must demonstrate financial need and societal impact potential. Deadline: 23 April 2026.
- **Commonwealth Master's Scholarship**: Full tuition + living expenses (£1,378-1,690/month) + clothing allowance. For Commonwealth countries — Brazil is NOT a Commonwealth country, so NOT eligible.
- **LSE Master's Awards (LMAs)**: £5,000 to full award. Priority for students from low/middle-income countries.
- **Graduate Support Scheme**: £5,000-£15,000 based on need.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** YES for AKO (Latin America); NO for Commonwealth.

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **MPA in Data Science for Public Policy** (2-year) — technical + policy skills for data-driven governance. HIGHLY RELEVANT.
- **MSc Media and Communications (Data and Society)** — critical skills in governing data systems, ethical and governance questions.
- **MSc Media and Communications (Media and Communication Governance)** — governance and regulation of digital platforms, press freedom, telecoms.
- **MSc International Social and Public Policy** — global challenges, poverty, migration.
- **Master of Public Policy (MPP)** — 9-month programme for mid-career professionals (5+ years experience). RELEVANT for the candidate's profile.

**Verdict:** TOP PICK. The AKO scholarship is explicitly for Latin Americans and covers full tuition + stipend. LSE has multiple highly relevant programmes. The MPP is ideal for a mid-career professional. Deadline is 23 April 2026 — still open.

### 4. LMU Munich (Germany) — QS #58

**Scholarships:**
- **Bavarian Study Scholarship** (via International Office): Based on academic excellence + financial need. Deadline: 15 January - 8 March 2026.
- **DAAD Scholarship**: Semester-long, for registered students at LMU. Similar deadline.
- **Deutschlandstipendium**: €300/month (not a full-ride).
- Germany has low/no tuition fees (~€150/semester administrative fee), which is a major advantage.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes.

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- LMU has strong AI ethics RESEARCH (Ethics of AI Governance research group 2023-2029, Junior Research Group in AI Ethics, Center for Ethics and Philosophy in Practice).
- However, there is NO specific English-taught master's programme in AI governance/ethics/policy.
- The Data Science master's has been DISCONTINUED.
- Philosophy and ethics programmes are mostly in German.

**Verdict:** Very low tuition is an advantage, but no specific English-taught master's programme matching the candidate's interests. Research environment is excellent but better suited for PhD. Not recommended for this search.

### 5. KU Leuven (Belgium) — QS #60

**Scholarships:**
- **Inspiring the Outstanding Scholarship**: FULL-RIDE. Covers full tuition waiver (only €136.50 admin fee) + €12,000/year living allowance. Can be for up to 2 years. Merit-based. Deadline: 15 January 2026 (LIKELY PASSED for 2026-2027).
- **Science@Leuven Scholarships**: Up to €12,000 for 1 year (Faculty of Science only). Deadline: 15 February 2026.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes.

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **Master of Artificial Intelligence** (1-year) — includes "AI Ethics & Regulation" course covering ethical and legal frameworks for AI development and use. Multidisciplinary (engineering, sciences, economics, psychology, linguistics). Taught in English.
- KU Leuven runs a prestigious **Summer School on the Law, Ethics and Policy of AI**.
- Research: Chair Ethics and AI, Leuven.AI institute.
- The AI master's is primarily technical but with ethics/regulation components.

**Verdict:** EXCELLENT full-ride (Inspiring the Outstanding). However, the January 2026 deadline has likely passed. The Master of AI is primarily technical — less focused on governance/policy than some alternatives. Worth checking if late applications are possible.

### 6. Université Paris-Saclay (France) — QS #70

**Scholarships:**
- **Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship**: €10,000/year (paid monthly Sept-June) + up to €1,000 for travel/visa. Must be under 30, first-time enrollment in France. Deadline: 31 March 2026 — STILL OPEN.
- **Eiffel Excellence Scholarship**: €1,200/month stipend + transport + insurance + housing assistance. Deadline: 8 January 2026 (institution submission) — PASSED.
- French universities have relatively low tuition for non-EU students (~€3,770/year for master's).
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes for both (Eiffel explicitly open to all non-French nationalities).

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **M2 Artificial Intelligence** — includes AI ethics and bias mitigation.
- **Master in Sustainability Science** (Science de la durabilité).
- **M2 Ethique, Sciences, Recherche et Société** (Ethics, Science, Research and Society).
- Nearby PSL (partner) offers **Master's in Artificial Intelligence and Society** with a "Governance and societal impact of AI" pathway — trains AI Ethics and Governance Specialists, Policy Advisors. HIGHLY RELEVANT.

**Verdict:** GOOD option. The Paris-Saclay scholarship (€10k/year) combined with low tuition makes it affordable but not a true full-ride. The PSL "AI and Society" master's is extremely relevant. The March 2026 deadline is still open for the Paris-Saclay scholarship.

### 7. Lund University (Sweden) — QS #72

**Scholarships:**
- **Lund University Global Scholarship**: Covers partial to full TUITION ONLY. 100+ scholarships awarded yearly (~SEK 20.5 million total). NO living costs coverage. Application via universityadmissions.se during 16 Oct 2025 - 15 Jan 2026 (PASSED).
- **Swedish Institute Scholarships for Global Professionals (SISGP)**: Covers tuition + living (SEK 10,000/month) + travel + insurance. For professionals from specific developing countries — check if Brazil is on the list.
- Sweden tuition for non-EU: SEK 100,000-205,000/year (~€9,000-18,500).
- Living costs: ~SEK 10,584/month minimum.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes for Lund Global; check SISGP country list.

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **"AI in Society"** (course SIMS40, Autumn 2026) — addresses fairness, responsibility, transparency; AI in law, health, democracy, climate policy. NOTE: This is a standalone COURSE, not a full master's degree.
- **Human Ecology — Culture, Power and Sustainability** (MSc) — sustainability focus.
- **Information Systems** (1-year master's).
- Lund is #1 globally in QS Sustainability Rankings 2026.

**Verdict:** No true full-ride (tuition only). The "AI in Society" is a course within another programme, not a standalone master's. SISGP would be the best funding route if Brazil is eligible. Limited programme fit for AI governance specifically.

### 8. Sorbonne University (France) — QS #72

**Scholarships:**
- **Eiffel Excellence Scholarship**: €1,200/month stipend + benefits. Institution-submitted. Deadline: 8 January 2026 (PASSED for 2026 intake).
- **MIEM Scholarship** (International Mobility in Master's): Monthly stipend for living expenses.
- French tuition for non-EU: ~€3,770/year for master's.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes.

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **MIND track** (Machine learning, AI and Data) — primarily technical.
- Sorbonne offers courses on ethical issues of AI.
- **Responsible AI** research initiative at SCAI (Sorbonne Center for AI).
- The PSL "AI and Society" master's (with Governance pathway) is accessible through the broader Paris ecosystem but is at PSL, not Sorbonne directly.

**Verdict:** Limited direct programme fit for AI governance/ethics (more technical focus). Eiffel deadline passed. Low tuition is an advantage. Not the best match among the options.

### 9. University of Warwick (UK) — QS #74

**Scholarships:**
- **Commonwealth Scholarships**: Full tuition + living allowance (£1,378-1,690/month) + clothing. Brazil is NOT a Commonwealth country — NOT ELIGIBLE.
- **GREAT Scholarships**: £15,000 toward tuition. Deadline: 22 April 2026. For all international students.
- **CIM Scholarships**: For digital media/data/AI programmes. South American applicants: ~£14,671 (50% of overseas tuition). RELEVANT.
- **Warwick Taught Masters Scholarship Scheme**: For UK students mainly.
- Overseas tuition estimated at ~£29,000-30,000/year.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Yes for GREAT and CIM; NO for Commonwealth.

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **MASc AI and Society** — HIGHLY RELEVANT. Interdisciplinary research on AI's impact on society; design, development, deployment and governance of AI systems; policy and governance implications. Provides skills to critically analyse AI's impacts.
- **MASc Global Sustainable Development** — sustainability focus.
- **MSc Applied Artificial Intelligence** — more technical.
- Warwick ranks 13th globally in QS Sustainability: Governance.

**Verdict:** EXCELLENT programme fit (AI and Society MASc). However, no true full-ride available for Brazilians. GREAT (£15k) + CIM (50% tuition) could stack but would still leave a gap. April 2026 deadline still open for GREAT.

### 10. Trinity College Dublin (Ireland) — QS #75

**Scholarships:**
- **Government of Ireland International Education Scholarship (GOI-IES)**: Full tuition waiver + €10,000 stipend. BUT only for students from **Low or Lower-Middle Income Countries** (World Bank classification). Brazil is classified as **Upper-Middle Income** — likely NOT eligible.
- **Global Excellence Postgraduate Scholarships**: €2,000-5,000 one-time tuition reduction. Deadline: 1 April 2026.
- International tuition: ~€26,989/year for MSc Computer Science.
- **Eligible for Brazilians:** Likely NOT for GOI-IES; YES for Global Excellence (small amount).

**Relevant Master's Programmes:**
- **MSc Computer Science — Intelligent Systems (AI)**: Technical focus on AI, NLP, data analytics. Less governance/policy focused.
- **AI and Ethics CPD course** (6 weeks online): Covers ethical, legal, political questions of AI. Not a degree programme.
- **AI Accountability Lab**: Strong research on AI transparency, justice, accountability. Led by Dr Abeba Birhane.

**Verdict:** Limited fit. The full scholarship (GOI-IES) likely excludes Brazil. The AI programmes are technically focused, not governance/policy. The research ecosystem (AI Accountability Lab) is interesting but better for PhD.
