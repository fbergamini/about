## 1. Imperial College London (UK) — QS #2

### Relevant Master's Programs
- **MSc Artificial Intelligence** — includes ethics of AI, fairness in ML, philosophical problems of AI
- **MSc AI Applications and Innovation** — ethical, social, and regulatory challenges of AI; entrepreneurship, ethics, and social impact
- **MSc Climate Change, Management & Finance** — interdisciplinary skills for climate/sustainability in business

### Full-Ride Scholarships

#### Chevening Scholarships (via British government)
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + monthly stipend for living + travel + visa fees
- **Brazil eligible:** YES (Brazil is a Chevening-eligible country)
- **Deadline:** Applications for 2026-27 CLOSED (was 7 October 2025). Next cycle opens ~August 2026 for 2027-28
- **Truly full-ride:** YES
- **Note:** Can be used at Imperial for any master's programme

#### CMA CGM Excellence Fund for Education
- **Coverage:** Full overseas tuition fees + £19,500/year living allowance
- **Brazil eligible:** NO — restricted to nationals from Least Developed Countries and Lower Middle Income Countries. Brazil is upper-middle-income
- **Truly full-ride:** Yes, but not available to Brazilians

#### GREAT Scholarship
- **Coverage:** £15,000 toward fees only
- **Brazil eligible:** NO — only for France, Egypt, Ghana, or Nigeria passport holders
- **Truly full-ride:** No (partial)

#### Imperial Commonwealth Scholarships
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + living costs
- **Brazil eligible:** NO — Brazil is not a Commonwealth country
- **Truly full-ride:** Yes, but not for Brazilians

### Verdict for Brazilian applicant
The only realistic full-ride path at Imperial is **Chevening** (next cycle: August 2026 for 2027-28 entry). No Imperial-specific full-ride scholarship is open to Brazilians.

## 2. University of Oxford (UK) — QS #4

### Relevant Master's Programs
- **MSc in Social Science of the Internet** (Oxford Internet Institute) — internet governance, digital methods, policy, tech regulation, AI governance. 10 months full-time. Highly relevant.
- **MPhil in Comparative Social Policy** — public policy dimension
- Oxford also has the **Institute for Ethics in AI** and the **OII Research Programme on AI, Government and Policy**, but the taught master's offering most aligned is the OII MSc.
- Note: The AI Ethics, Regulation and Compliance Programme is executive education (short course), not a full master's degree.

### Full-Ride Scholarships

#### Clarendon Scholarship
- **Coverage:** Full tuition fees (Home or Overseas) + living grant of at least £15,009/year
- **Brazil eligible:** YES — no nationality restrictions
- **Deadline:** Automatic consideration if you apply by 2 December 2025 or 8 January 2026 (for 2026-27 entry). No separate application
- **Truly full-ride:** YES
- **Competitiveness:** ~140 awards per year across all graduate courses

#### Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships and Leadership Programme
- **Coverage:** 100% tuition + living grant of at least £19,237 + leadership programme
- **Brazil eligible:** YES — Brazil is explicitly listed as an eligible country
- **Deadline:** Select in funding section of graduate application by Dec/Jan deadline (2 Dec 2025 or 8 Jan 2026)
- **Truly full-ride:** YES
- **Eligible courses:** MSc Social Science of the Internet IS an eligible course
- **Note:** Must intend to return to home country after studies

#### Said Foundation Oxford Scholarships
- **Coverage:** Tuition + living grant (at least £20,780) + flights
- **Brazil eligible:** Primarily for nationals of Syria, Jordan, Lebanon, Palestine, and Turkey. NOT for Brazilians

#### Chevening Scholarships
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + living + travel + visa
- **Brazil eligible:** YES
- **Deadline:** CLOSED for 2026-27 (was October 2025). Next cycle opens August 2026

### Verdict for Brazilian applicant
Oxford is EXCELLENT. Two strong full-ride options: **Clarendon** and **Weidenfeld-Hoffmann** (both open to Brazilians, both truly full-ride). The MSc Social Science of the Internet at OII is a near-perfect match for AI governance/policy/digital rights interests. Deadlines for 2026-27 entry have passed (Dec 2025/Jan 2026), so target **2027-28 entry** with Dec 2026/Jan 2027 deadlines.

## 3. University of Cambridge (UK) — QS #6

### Relevant Master's Programs
- **MPhil in Ethics of AI, Data and Algorithms** — 9 months, full-time. Run by the Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence. Covers ethics, fairness, bias, surveillance, responsible innovation, privacy. HIGHLY RELEVANT.
- **MSt in AI Ethics and Society** — part-time, 2 years. For working professionals. Covers privacy, surveillance, justice, fairness, algorithmic bias, responsible innovation. Also highly relevant but requires continuing employment.

### Full-Ride Scholarships

#### Gates Cambridge Scholarship
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + £21,000/year stipend + health insurance + up to £2,000 academic development + airfare + visa costs + family allowance (up to £16,548)
- **Brazil eligible:** YES — open to all non-UK citizens
- **Deadline:** 2 December 2025 or 7 January 2026 (for 2026-27 entry, now passed). Next cycle ~Dec 2026/Jan 2027
- **Truly full-ride:** YES — one of the most generous scholarships in the world
- **Awards:** ~80/year total (~55 in international round), about 1/3 for master's (~25-27 master's awards)
- **How to apply:** Apply through University's Graduate Application Portal; no separate application

#### Chevening Scholarships
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + living + travel + visa
- **Brazil eligible:** YES
- **Deadline:** CLOSED for 2026-27

### Verdict for Brazilian applicant
Cambridge is EXCELLENT. The **MPhil in Ethics of AI, Data and Algorithms** is one of the best-matched programs anywhere for the candidate's interests. The **Gates Cambridge** is a truly full-ride, prestigious scholarship open to Brazilians. Target **2027-28 entry** with Dec 2026/Jan 2027 deadlines.

## 4. ETH Zurich (Switzerland) — QS #7

### Relevant Master's Programs
- **MSc in Science, Technology and Policy (STP)** — addresses climate change, sustainable energy, digital society, and policy. Combines engineering/science depth with policy analysis skills. 4 semesters. HIGHLY RELEVANT.
- **Master in Comparative and International Studies (MACIS)** — politics, governance, international relations

### Full-Ride Scholarships

#### Excellence Scholarship & Opportunity Programme (ESOP)
- **Coverage:** Tuition fee waiver + CHF 12,000/semester for living and study expenses (~CHF 48,000 total for 4 semesters) + mentorship + professional network access
- **Brazil eligible:** YES — open to all nationalities
- **Deadline:** Application window: 1-30 November 2026 for start in autumn 2027
- **Truly full-ride:** YES — tuition waiver + living stipend
- **Awards:** ~60 per year
- **Competitiveness:** Must be in top 10% of bachelor's programme
- **How to apply:** Apply through the regular master's admission process + submit a pre-proposal for Master's thesis

#### ETH-D Scholarship
- **Coverage:** Similar to ESOP (tuition waiver + living grant)
- **Brazil eligible:** YES

### Verdict for Brazilian applicant
ETH Zurich is a STRONG option. The MSc in Science, Technology and Policy is an excellent match. ESOP is truly full-ride. November 2026 deadline for autumn 2027 entry.

## 5. UCL — University College London (UK) — QS #9

### Relevant Master's Programs
- **MSc Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Development** — combines AI with environmental/humanitarian issues, transparent and responsible AI. 1 year. HIGHLY RELEVANT.
- **MPA Digital Technologies and Policy** — digital governance, cybersecurity, data protection, algorithmic integrity, digital rights, responsible innovation. HIGHLY RELEVANT.

### Full-Ride Scholarships

#### UCL Global Master's Scholarship
- **Coverage:** £15,000 (applied first to tuition, remainder to living costs)
- **Brazil eligible:** YES — open to all non-UK nationals
- **Deadline:** 7 May 2026 (for 2026-27)
- **Truly full-ride:** NO — £15,000 is partial. Overseas tuition alone is typically £30,000-35,000
- **Awards:** up to 85

#### Chevening Scholarships
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + living + travel + visa
- **Brazil eligible:** YES
- **Deadline:** CLOSED for 2026-27

#### Other UCL scholarships
- Various department-specific scholarships exist but most are partial (£5,000-£10,000)

### Verdict for Brazilian applicant
UCL has two EXCELLENT program matches (AI for Sustainable Development and Digital Technologies & Policy), but **lacks its own full-ride scholarship** for Brazilians. The only full-ride path is **Chevening** (next cycle August 2026). The UCL Global Master's Scholarship is helpful but not full-ride.

## 6. EPFL — Ecole Polytechnique Federale de Lausanne (Switzerland) — QS #22

### Relevant Master's Programs
- **MSc in Digital Humanities** — bridging technology with cultural/social understanding, user experience, interdisciplinary
- **MSc in Sustainable Management and Technology** — jointly with UNIL-HEC and IMD. Combines technology, innovation, management, and sustainability. Relevant but more business-oriented.
- Note: EPFL is primarily a technical/engineering school. Less direct coverage of AI ethics/governance/policy compared to Oxford or Cambridge.

### Full-Ride Scholarships

#### EPFL Excellence Fellowships
- **Coverage:** CHF 10,000/semester (up to 4 semesters = CHF 40,000 total) + guaranteed student housing reservation
- **Brazil eligible:** YES — open to all nationalities
- **Deadline:** 15 December 2025 or 30 April 2026 (for September 2026 start)
- **Truly full-ride:** PARTIAL — the CHF 10,000/semester must cover BOTH tuition (~CHF 730/semester) and living costs. Living costs in Lausanne are ~CHF 1,800-2,200/month, so CHF 10,000/semester covers ~5 months of basic living + tuition. It helps significantly but may not cover everything.
- **Awards:** ~3% of master's students
- **Note:** EPFL tuition is very low (~CHF 730/semester), so the fellowship mainly covers living expenses

### Verdict for Brazilian applicant
EPFL's fellowship is generous relative to its low tuition, effectively covering most costs. However, the program alignment is weaker than Oxford/Cambridge/ETH for AI ethics/governance topics. Best if combining technical AI skills with sustainability interests.

## 7. Technical University of Munich — TUM (Germany) — QS #22

### Relevant Master's Programs
- **MSc AI in Society** — covers AI governance, law and regulation, human-AI interaction, AI in diverse societies, ethical aspects. Interdisciplinary. HIGHLY RELEVANT. Taught in English.
- **MSc Sustainable Management and Technology** — biotechnology, sustainability, circular economy

### Full-Ride Scholarships

#### DAAD Scholarships (German Academic Exchange Service)
- **Coverage:** ~€992/month stipend + health insurance + travel allowance + sometimes tuition waiver
- **Brazil eligible:** YES
- **Deadline:** Typically October 31 of previous year (October 2025 for 2026-27 start — CLOSED). Next cycle ~October 2026
- **Truly full-ride:** YES (with tuition waiver option). Monthly stipend covers living in Munich modestly
- **Note:** DAAD has specific programs for STEM and for developing countries

#### TUM International Student Scholarship
- **Coverage:** €500-€1,800/semester (one-time, reapplyable)
- **Truly full-ride:** NO — this is very partial

#### TUM Tuition Fee Waiver
- **Coverage:** Waiver of the €4,000/semester tuition for non-EEA students
- **Brazil eligible:** YES — can apply for waiver
- **Truly full-ride:** No (only tuition waiver, no living costs)

#### Deutschlandstipendium
- **Coverage:** €300/month (€3,600/year)
- **Truly full-ride:** NO — partial supplement

### Verdict for Brazilian applicant
TUM's **MSc AI in Society** is one of the BEST program matches across all universities for the candidate's interests. However, TUM lacks its own full-ride scholarship. Best strategy: **DAAD scholarship + TUM tuition waiver** combined. DAAD deadline for next cycle: ~October 2026 for 2027-28 entry. TUM MSc application deadline: 31 May.

## Cross-Cutting Scholarships (applicable to multiple UK universities)

### Chevening Scholarships (UK Government)
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + living expenses (~£1,133/month in London, £1,009 elsewhere) + travel + visa + thesis grant
- **Brazil eligible:** YES
- **Deadline:** CLOSED for 2026-27 (was 7 Oct 2025). Opens ~August 2026 for 2027-28
- **Applies to:** Any UK university (Imperial, Oxford, Cambridge, UCL)
- **Truly full-ride:** YES

## Ranking of Options by Fit and Feasibility

### Tier 1 — Best Program Match + Full-Ride Available to Brazilians

1. **Cambridge — MPhil in Ethics of AI, Data and Algorithms + Gates Cambridge Scholarship**
   - Near-perfect program match. Gates is one of the world's best scholarships. Open to Brazilians. Target Dec 2026/Jan 2027 deadline.

2. **Oxford — MSc Social Science of the Internet (OII) + Clarendon or Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarship**
   - Excellent program match (AI governance, digital rights, internet regulation). Two full-ride options, both open to Brazilians. Target Dec 2026/Jan 2027 deadline.

3. **ETH Zurich — MSc Science, Technology and Policy + ESOP Scholarship**
   - Strong match for tech policy/climate/sustainability. ESOP is truly full-ride. Target November 2026 deadline.

### Tier 2 — Excellent Program Match, Scholarship Path More Complex

4. **TUM — MSc AI in Society + DAAD Scholarship**
   - One of the best-matched programs (AI governance, regulation, ethics, society). Need to combine DAAD + tuition waiver. Target October 2026 (DAAD) + May 2027 (TUM).

5. **UCL — MSc AI for Sustainable Development or MPA Digital Technologies & Policy + Chevening**
   - Two great programs. But UCL's own scholarships are partial. Chevening is the full-ride path. Target August 2026 Chevening opening.

### Tier 3 — Good University, Weaker Program or Scholarship Match

6. **Imperial — MSc AI or MSc AI Applications & Innovation + Chevening**
   - Strong AI programs with ethics modules but less focused on governance/policy/social impact than the options above. No Imperial-specific full-ride for Brazilians.

7. **EPFL — MSc Digital Humanities or Sustainable Management & Technology + Excellence Fellowship**
   - Fellowship is generous but program alignment is weaker for AI ethics/governance/policy specifically.
