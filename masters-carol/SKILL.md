# Masters Program Research Skill

You are helping Carol, a Brazilian psychologist and social entrepreneur with 8+ years of experience at the intersection of mental health, social justice, sustainability, and community empowerment, find full-ride master's programs in Europe.

## Profile Summary

- Brazilian citizen, needs scholarships open to Brazilians
- BSc in Psychology (PUC-SP — Pontifícia Universidade Católica de São Paulo)
- 8+ years professional experience working where mental health, social justice, sustainability, and community empowerment intersect
- Co-founder and Executive Director of Asas Para Voar, an ecological house promoting sustainable actions in the city
- Awarded the International Marcus Matraga Prize (2019) by Unión Latinoamericana de Entidades de Psicología for her commitment to reducing social inequality
- Received funding from Fundação Tide Setubal to expand clinical-social programs in marginalized communities
- Global leadership training: Harvard Aspire Leaders, Youth Climate Leaders, AIESEC Mumbai, United People Global, YOUNGO (UNFCCC)
- Direct impact on 2,000+ people, especially youth and women in vulnerable contexts
- Extensive experience with grassroots movements, NGOs, and public policy advocacy
- Designs evidence-based solutions and community-led initiatives

## What She's Looking For

### Core Research Interest

An international master's exploring the relationship between **Nature and subjectivity** — investigating how engagement with Nature can mobilize climate action and sustainable development in socio-environmentally vulnerable communities. She wants to explore the intersections with **Ecopsychology** tools to create local and global impact through community engagement.

### Program Topics (must explicitly address at least 2)

- Ecopsychology / environmental psychology
- Nature-based interventions and well-being
- Climate psychology and climate action
- Sustainable community development
- Environmental justice and social-environmental vulnerability
- Community engagement and participatory approaches
- Nature connectedness and human-nature relationships
- Sustainability and regenerative practices
- Psychology of climate change and ecological crisis
- Social-ecological systems and resilience
- Environmental humanities (with psychology/community focus)
- Public health and nature / planetary health

### NOT Interested In

- Pure clinical psychology programs without an environmental or community dimension
- Pure environmental science without a human/psychological/community component
- MBA or business-focused programs
- Programs focused solely on policy without a community or psychological lens
- Pure conservation biology without social/human dimensions

### Scholarship Requirements

- Must cover tuition AND living costs (full ride)
- Must be open to Brazilian applicants
- Programs taught in English or Portuguese

### Study Format

- In-person or hybrid only (no fully remote — networking matters)
- Duration: 1 or 2 years, no preference

## How to Use This Skill

This skill is designed to be run **once per scholarship program**. Each run should focus on one specific scholarship and find the best matching master's programs within it.

The scholarship programs to research are listed in `masters-carol/scholarships.md`. Check that file for the next scholarship to research.

### Step 1: Research the Scholarship

When invoked, research the specified scholarship program by searching the web for:
- Official website and current edition details
- Eligibility requirements for Brazilian applicants
- What it covers (tuition, living costs, travel, insurance, etc.)
- Application deadlines and timeline
- Required documents and application process
- Selection criteria and competitiveness
- Number of scholarships awarded annually
- Any restrictions (age, field of study, university list, etc.)

### Step 2: Find Matching Programs

Within the scholarship's eligible universities/programs, search for master's programs that match Carol's interests (see "Program Topics" above). For each program found, research:
- Country, university ranking (QS/THE if available), duration, language, format (in-person/hybrid)
- Program URL
- Why it matches — which topics from the list it covers
- Key courses (list 3-5 most relevant in the curriculum)
- Notable faculty/research groups (if findable)
- Alumni/career outcomes (if available)

### Step 3: Rank and Recommend

After listing all matching programs, provide a ranked recommendation considering:
1. **Alignment with interests** — How well does the program match the topics listed above? Prioritize programs that bridge psychology/subjectivity with nature/climate/sustainability
2. **University reputation** — Global ranking and reputation in the specific field
3. **Scholarship fit** — How competitive is Carol's profile for this specific scholarship + program combo? (Her social impact track record, awards, and global leadership experience are strong differentiators)
4. **Practical factors** — Cost of living in the city, language, community for Brazilians

Create a file at `masters-carol/programs/<scholarship-slug>.md` with all findings using this template:

- **Notes:** [anything else relevant]

- Add any new scholarships discovered during research to the list
