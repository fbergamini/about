# Swedish Institute Scholarships for Global Professionals (SISGP)

## Scholarship Overview

- **Website:** https://si.se/en/apply/scholarships/swedish-institute-scholarships-for-global-professionals/
- **Coverage:** Full tuition fees + monthly living allowance of SEK 12,000 (~€1,050/month) + travel grant of SEK 15,000
- **Eligibility for Brazilians:** Yes — Brazil is among the 33+ eligible countries
- **Deadline:** Apply to master's programmes via UniversityAdmissions.se by 15 January 2026; SI scholarship application portal open 9–25 February 2026
- **Duration:** 1 or 2 years (depends on the programme chosen)
- **Documents Required:** CV, two letters of reference (one work-based, one from work or civil society engagement), motivation statement, proof of work/leadership experience (using SI templates)
- **Selection Criteria:** Leadership experience (professional or civil society), clear vision for how studies contribute to sustainable development in home country/region, demonstrated work experience (minimum 3,000 hours), alignment with sustainability goals
- **Notes:** ~700 eligible English-taught programmes across Swedish universities. About 400 scholarships awarded annually. Results announced 23 April 2026; acceptance deadline 30 May 2026. Carol's profile is very competitive: 8+ years of social impact work, international awards, global leadership experience (Harvard, UNFCCC/YOUNGO), and strong sustainability narrative.

## Matching Programs

### 1. Social-Ecological Resilience for Sustainable Development — Stockholm University

- **Country:** Sweden
- **University Ranking:** Stockholm University — QS #137 (2025); Stockholm Resilience Centre is world-leading in sustainability science
- **Duration:** 2 years (120 ECTS)
- **Language:** English
- **Format:** In-person, full-time, daytime instruction in Stockholm
- **Program URL:** https://www.stockholmresilience.org/education/graduate-level-masters.html
- **Why it matches:** Directly addresses **social-ecological systems and resilience**, **community and governance**, and **human-nature relationships**. The programme explores how ecological processes support human wellbeing and how communities can govern natural resources sustainably. Carol's work with vulnerable communities and climate action aligns perfectly with the resilience thinking framework. Only 18 students/year — intimate, research-intensive cohort.
- **Key courses:**
  - Social-Ecological Systems: Challenges and Approaches
  - Systems Theory and Resilience Thinking
  - Governance and Management of Social-Ecological Systems
  - Resilience Reflections and Applications
  - Master's thesis aligned with Stockholm Resilience Centre research themes
- **Notable faculty/research groups:** Stockholm Resilience Centre (founded by the late Johan Rockström, now led by Carl Folke) — pioneers of planetary boundaries framework and resilience theory. Research themes include biosphere stewardship, governance, and transformations to sustainability.
- **Alumni/career outcomes:** PhD programmes, government ministries, NGOs, research institutes, indigenous rights organizations, consulting agencies worldwide.

- **Language:** English
- **Format:** In-person, full-time

Examines climate justice, environmental inequality, ecological transitions, and the interconnections between humans, societies, and environments. - **Notable faculty/research groups:** Department of Human Geography, with researchers in political ecology, environmental humanities, and sustainability science. LUCSUS (Lund University Centre for Sustainability Studies) is closely affiliated.
